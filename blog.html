How to Index Your Vercel Website on Google (Step-by-Step)

Category: SEO | Date: 2026-01-20 | Author: Mayank Rawat

Tags: SEO, Google Search Console, Vercel, Indexing, Sitemap

Intro

If your website is hosted on Vercel and it is not showing on Google, the easiest solution is Google Search Console. It helps Google discover and index your website properly.

Steps

1) Open Google Search Console

  • Go to Google Search Console
  • Add your website property

2) Add Property (Choose one option)

  • Domain Property: Covers all subdomains and is recommended
  • URL Prefix: Easy for beginners, example: https://yourwebsite.vercel.app/

3) Verify Ownership

  • Use DNS verification (best for Vercel websites)
  • Alternative options: Google Analytics or Tag Manager (optional)

4) Submit Sitemap

  • Go to Search Console → Sitemaps
  • Submit: https://yourwebsite.vercel.app/sitemap.xml

5) Request Indexing

  • Go to URL Inspection
  • Paste your page URL and click Request Indexing

Bonus Tips

  • Add internal links between sections/pages
  • Share your website on social media
  • Build backlinks from relevant platforms
  • Improve page speed and mobile usability

Conclusion

Indexing a Vercel website is simple if you set up Google Search Console, submit your sitemap, and request indexing through the URL Inspection tool.

Robots.txt and Sitemap.xml Explained (With Best Example)

Category: SEO | Date: 2026-01-20 | Author: Mayank Rawat

Tags: SEO, Robots.txt, Sitemap.xml, Crawling, Indexing

What is Sitemap.xml?

A sitemap.xml file tells search engines which pages exist on your website and helps them discover important pages faster. 



  
    https://yourwebsite.vercel.app/
    2026-01-20
    weekly
    1.0

What is Robots.txt?

A robots.txt file tells search engine bots which pages they are allowed to crawl and where your sitemap is located. 

User-agent: *
Allow: /

Sitemap: https://yourwebsite.vercel.app/sitemap.xml

Why Important?

  • Helps Google crawl your website properly
  • Improves indexing speed for new pages
  • Shows that your website follows SEO best practices
  • Makes the site more professional for search engines

Conclusion

For a static HTML/CSS/JS website, adding robots.txt and sitemap.xml is one of the best basic SEO steps. After adding them, submit your sitemap in Google Search Console.
